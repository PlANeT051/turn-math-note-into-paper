## Modifications in this skill

The upstream four-skill, Claude-oriented suite was condensed into one Codex skill for theoretical mathematics. The adaptation:

- replaces generic empirical-evidence hierarchies with a mathematics-specific source and theorem-verification ladder;
- merges research, writing, review, revision, and re-review handoffs into the existing contribution, dependency, proof, TeX, and longitudinal-audit workflow;
- rewrites the integrity failure modes around proof steps, theorem scope, source versions, novelty, optimality, and revision drift;
- retains read-only referee review, comment coverage, commitment tracking, response verification, counter-evidence search, and style calibration;
- omits Claude-specific hooks, slash commands, environment variables, agent-card machinery, model-routing contracts, and generic PRISMA/statistical procedures that do not apply to a mathematics manuscript;
- adds Douglas B. West Rules 1-92, semantic TeX auditing, graph-theory terminology, and author-specific house style from this skill's independent materials.
